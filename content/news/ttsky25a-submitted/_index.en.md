We just packed 237 of your projects into our most exciting ASIC to date! Let's take a look inside…

### Asteroid RISC-V Takes Flight

This was the first time we enabled 4-tile high designs, and it opened the door to something special: [Asteroid](https://tinytapeout.com/chips/ttsky25a/tt_um_tt_tinyQV), a monster crowdsourced RISC-V microcontroller. At 60,000 standard cells, it's the biggest design we've ever seen on Tiny Tapeout. We'll be announcing the RISC-V competition results in the next few days!

### Analog Innovation

Inspired by Peter Kinget, we commissioned Andrew Kang to create a [Mini MOSBius](https://tinytapeout.com/chips/ttsky25a/tt_um_mosbius) circuit especially for us. The idea is brilliant! An analog FPGA that lets analog designers get faster feedback on their circuit designs, right on real silicon.

### Tributes and Community

Mitch Altman has long inspired us, so as a tribute we included an ASIC version of his classic [TV-B-Gone](https://tinytapeout.com/chips/ttsky25a/tt_um_tv_b_gone_rom) — the universal off button for TVs. We also took the opportunity to test Sylvain Munaut's ROM.

We have our first commissioned art piece from Sabine Wieluch: ["Cross Stitch Creatures"](https://tinytapeout.com/chips/ttsky25a/tt_um_bleeptrack_cc2) — the first of a 4-part series exploring what silicon art looks like.

### Mixed-Signal Maturity

It's fantastic to see people using our service to test bigger, more ambitious mixed-signal designs. Jesse put a tiny 16×32 [OpenRAM](https://tinytapeout.com/chips/ttsky25a/tt_um_openram_top) memory block inside a 4-tile digital design. 

Another designer created a [PLL](https://tinytapeout.com/chips/ttsky25a/tt_um_tiny_pll) that takes full advantage of our smallest and cheapest tile — with excellent documentation too! And Anton Maurovic designed a beautiful current steering [DAC](https://tinytapeout.com/chips/ttsky25a/tt_um_algofoogle_vga_matrix_dac) optimized to drive VGA monitors. Great to see your mixed-signal skills evolving, Anton!

### What's Next

Thanks to [ChipFoundry](https://chipfoundry.io) for sponsoring us and keeping ASIC design and manufacturing affordable! Our next shuttle is already open, and thanks to ChipFoundry you can tape out a single-tile digital design and receive your own ASIC for just €185 including shipping.

For all the details, check out the interactive viewer and the full datasheet [here](https://tinytapeout.com/chips/ttsky25a/).
