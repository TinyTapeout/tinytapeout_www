Our [latest chip](/chips/ttsky25b/) is on the way to the foundry!

[TTSKY25b](/chips/ttsky25b/) is packed to the gills. At [98% utilization](/chips/ttsky25b/#chip-map), every scrap of silicon is being used. This shuttle closed on November 10th with 316 designs, a staggering amount of community creativity crammed into one tapeout.

Among the designs is a [WSPR radio transmitter](/chips/ttsky25b/tt_um_cejmu_wspr) for long-distance amateur radio communications. A [tiny GPU](/chips/ttsky25b/tt_um_pongsagon_tinygpu_v2) — actual graphics acceleration on an open source ASIC. And most impressively, a [uLinux-capable RISC-V SoC](/chips/ttsky25b/tt_um_kianV_rv32ima_uLinux_SoC): a complete processor capable of running actual Linux.

Big thanks to [ChipFoundry](https://chipfoundry.io/) for sponsoring this shuttle. Their support keeps the momentum going and makes these ambitious projects possible.

For all the details on TTSKY25b, check out the datasheet and interactive chip map [here](/chips/ttsky25b).
