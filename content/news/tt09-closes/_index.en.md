Tiny Tapeout 9 is closed and we broke our previous record!

[369 projects](/chips/tt09/) were submitted. We filled 95% of the available area with an incredible range of designs that showcase the breadth of what's possible when chip design becomes accessible!

On the digital side, standout projects include an [Atari 2600](/chips/tt09/tt_um_rejunity_atari2600) (complete with games), a wide range of CPUs and RISC-V cores, and several SDR projects. Gaming and entertainment designs also made the cut, including a 2048 sliding tile puzzle game with VGA output, Simon Says memory game, and a maze explorer game.

On the analog and mixed-signal side, designers pushed the boundaries with [PLLs](/chips/tt09/tt_um_mickey_pll), [opamps](/chips/tt09/tt_um_rburt16_opamp_3stage), [ADCs](/chips/tt09/tt_um_asheldon44_dsm_decimation_filter), [DACs](/chips/tt09/tt_um_mattvenn_r2r_dac_3v3), [time-to-digital converters](/chips/tt09/tt_um_13hihi31_tdc), classic 8-bit era Programmable Sound Generators ([SN76489](/chips/tt09/tt_um_rejunity_sn76489) and [AY-3-8913](/chips/tt09/tt_um_rejunity_ay8913)), and even [ring oscillators](/chips/tt09/tt_um_urish_giant_ringosc).

Even more ambitious: neuromorphic designs spanning [spiking neural networks](/chips/tt09/tt_um_snn_with_delays_paolaunisa), [Hopfield neural networks](/chips/tt09/tt_um_cellular_alchemist), and [leaky integrate-and-fire neurons](/chips/tt09/tt_um_lif_sfiguerr).

For all the details, head over to the [TT09 chip page](/chips/tt09/) or read the [700-page datasheet](https://tinytapeout.github.io/tinytapeout-09/datasheet.pdf).

If you missed out on this one, don't worry! [TT10](/chips/tt10/) is already open for submissions and closes March 7th.
