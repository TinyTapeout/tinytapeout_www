We did it! 

We sold all the tiles on [TT06](/chips/tt06) - 238 projects submitted from 30 countries.

Across all projects, that's 9 meters of wire and 267,000 standard cells. The highest utilisation was 93%, achieved by Roland's [Simplez design](/chips/tt06/740), and the biggest single project was Luca's NIST [random number generator test](/chips/tt06/842) with 19,265 cells. Building all the projects took 17 hours.

Verilog was the most popular language with 147 projects, followed by Wokwi with 41 and SystemVerilog with 11. The rest used a mix of other HDLs, including quite a few in Chisel. The [datasheet](https://tinytapeout.github.io/tinytapeout-06/datasheet.pdf) documenting it all runs to 560 pages!

We sold 100% of the Efabless-sponsored PCBs, plus another 60 at full price. 

Thanks to [Efabless](https://chipfoundry.io/efabless) for sponsoring us and making this project possible!

24 analog and mixed-signal designs were submitted, including [opamps](/chips/tt06/271), [ADCs](/chips/tt06/482), [DACs](/chips/tt06/558), and a [555 timer](/chips/tt06/267), among others, taking advantage of TT06's new analog capabilities.

And more than 40 projects came in on the very last day!
